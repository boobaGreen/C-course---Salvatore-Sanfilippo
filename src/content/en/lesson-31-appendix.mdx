## Beyond Curriculum Learning

Salvatore decides to remove **Curriculum Learning** (which previously forced the program to learn the bitmap first and then the colors). Thanks to the speed achieved by the O(1) algorithm developed in the previous lesson, the program can now evolve bitmaps and attributes simultaneously.

<Callout type="info" title="Speed and Convergence">
  Without the curriculum constraint, the program "discovers" the color and pixel
  combination at the same time. While this can lead to running into **local
  minima** (non-optimal solutions where the algorithm gets stuck), the speed is
  such that simply relaunching the process a couple of times is enough to get an
  excellent result.
</Callout>

---

## The Problem of Dyschromia

An image may be technically "close" to the original but appear unattractive due to adjacent blocks having very different colors (**dyschromia**).

Salvatore introduces the concept of **Color Bonus**:

- Better scores are given to images that maintain chromatic coherence between adjacent squares.
- This prevents the algorithm from making "selfish" choices at a local level that ruin the global aesthetics.

<Callout type="pro" title="Human Guidance">
  AI does not "see" the image as we do. Salvatore had to intuit that the
  aesthetic problem was linked to the lack of coherence between blocks and
  instructed the AI to implement a system of rewards (bonuses) for coherent
  colors.
</Callout>

## Saturation and Spectrum Palette

Since the ZX Spectrum has a very bright and limited palette of colors, an input image that is too "soft" or realistic can look dull.

To solve this, Salvatore implements:

1. **Saturation Boost**: Increases the saturation of the source image before processing it.
2. **Bright/Normal Toggle**: The ability to force the use of only "Bright" colors or only normal ones to achieve a more vibrant look.

---

## Toward Real Hardware: TAP Files

The ultimate goal of this suite of tools is to be able to enjoy the result on a real ZX Spectrum. The program has been updated to generate **.TAP** files.

<Callout type="warning" title="Dithering and CRT">
  On old cathode ray tube (CRT) monitors, the *dithering* effect (those close
  dots) is not seen as a grid, but blends into a compact and natural tint. This
  is where the evolution algorithm shows all its power.
</Callout>
