## Signal Physics: The TAP Format

To load the image onto a real ZX Spectrum, we must create a **.TAP** file. This file is a digital representation of what would have been recorded onto a **magnetic cassette** in 1982.

<Callout type="pro" title="Anatomy of a TAP File">
  A TAP data block consists of: - **Header (19 bytes)**: Contains the file name
  and dump size. - **Data Block**: The bytes of our image. - **Checksum**: A
  final byte (XOR of all previous bytes) to verify that the "tape reading"
  didn't introduce errors (background noise).
</Callout>

## The CRT Monitor as an Analog Filter

Why does dithering look so good on an old TV?

- **Persistence**: CRT phosphors continue to emit light for a few milliseconds after the electron beam passes.
- **Beam Focus**: The electron beam isn't a perfect square like an LCD pixel; it has a soft edge that performs **natural Anti-Aliasing**.

<Callout type="info" title="The Final Secret">
  Salvatore's evolution algorithm exploits this hardware blur to create images
  that look almost like "true color" despite the machine's extreme limits.
</Callout>
